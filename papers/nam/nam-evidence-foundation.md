## ORIGINAL CONTRIBUTIONS — GAPS IN THE LITERATURE

These are areas where NAM makes a claim that is not directly supported by a single existing paper. Flagged as original contributions and future research directions.

**1. The ACCESS question as physiological event.** NAM claims the ACCESS question produces a physiological shift within seconds. This is theoretically supported by Barrett (2017) constructed-emotion theory but no study directly measures autonomic change within seconds of a single verbal cue in a coaching context. This is a real gap and a future study opportunity.

**2. Pre-myelinated procedural pathways as the stress-surviving route.** This is NAM's original theoretical synthesis combining (a) procedural memory's stress resistance (Schwabe et al., 2012), (b) implicit motor learning preserved post-stroke (Kal et al., 2016), and (c) activity-dependent myelination as the automaticity substrate (Bonetto et al., 2021). No single paper makes this exact synthesis. Flag as NAM's original contribution.

**3. Constraint-led coaching of adaptive/para-sport populations.** Allan et al. (2020) is the only direct bridge from Newell's constraints-led approach to para-sport development. This is a thin empirical area and a publication opportunity for follow-up NAM research.
